What is DNA? What is it used for?

DNA is a tiny molecule that is found in nearly all cells in our body. Similar to computer code, it contains the instructions for building the proteins that make up our body.


The human genome contains some 6.4 billion base pairs(!). Sequencing the first human genome originally cost over $3 billion. Today, that price has been brought down to below $300. We've come a long way!
